How I Work

01

Uncovering the Essence

Seeing what is true beneath the noise.

Beneath every thought, feeling, action, and story there is something essential — a deeper truth about who you are and what is really happening in your life. Sometimes that truth belongs to you personally. Sometimes it reflects a deeper reality we all share. Either way, it can become buried beneath confusion, habit, or the noise of everyday life. My work is to help you move past that noise and rediscover what is true.

02

Shaping the Story

Transforming the narratives that shape you.

The stories we tell ourselves shape the lives we live. Some stories keep us stuck, quietly limiting what we believe is possible. Others open space for growth, freedom, and change. Together we examine the narratives you carry and reshape them so your life begins to move in alignment with who you truly are.

03

Living Compassion

Seeing the pain beneath behaviour and meeting it with compassion.

Compassion is not just an approach to me — it is the way I see people. Karuna means compassion in Sanskrit. It reflects a quality that comes naturally to me: the ability to look beneath behaviour and recognize the pain that gives rise to it. When a person feels truly seen in this way, self-compassion can take root and shame begins to dissolve.

04

Leading with Honesty

Creating the courage to speak truthfully and live authentically.

Honesty takes courage. I have learned that when one person speaks openly from the heart, it gives others permission to do the same. In our work together I lead with that honesty — creating a space where truth can be spoken, vulnerability is welcomed, and real connection can begin.

The Roots of My Work

The way I work is based on who I am - the qualities that remain in me despite change. My approach grows out of an ongoing personal exploration of life, relationships, and the deeper patterns that shape how we live.

Over the years my search has carried me through many different experiences and expressions of the human condition — spiritual practice, creative work, adventure, and countless conversations about what it means to be human.

Each step has deepened my curiosity and sharpened my ability to listen beneath the surface of things. In time, that path naturally led me to counselling.

Professional Training

I hold a degree in counselling from The South African College of Applied Psychology (SACAP).

For five years I worked full-time as a Relationship Coach on the American coaching platform Relationship Hero, supporting individuals and couples navigating complex emotional and relational challenges.

In addition, I completed a rigorous 720-hour training programme in somatic healing modalities, deepening my understanding of how emotional experience lives within the body.

Alongside my formal training, I maintain an ongoing commitment to learning, reflection, and personal development.

My approach integrates professional training with insights gained through lived experience, contemplative practice, and many years of exploring what it means to be human.
